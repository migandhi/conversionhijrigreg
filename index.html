Limitations and Accuracy

This converter uses a tabular (arithmetic) Islamic calendar. It does not rely on the physical sighting of the moon. Here are some key points to consider:

  • Astronomical vs. Sighting: The calculated Hijri date is based on a fixed mathematical algorithm. It may differ by one to two days from the official date in your country, which is often determined by the actual sighting of the crescent moon. [1]
  • Purpose: This tool is excellent for historical, administrative, and planning purposes where a predictable calendar is needed. [1] For determining the start of religious events like Ramadan or Eid, you should always refer to your local religious authorities.
  • Accuracy Over Time: The algorithm maintains mathematical consistency for a very wide range of years. However, like any algorithmic calendar, it may slowly diverge from the actual lunar cycle over very long periods (e.g., one day over approximately 2500 years). [4, 6]
